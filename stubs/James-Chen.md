**James Chen**

Former Member, Meat Mind Collective (2023-2026)

**Professional Summary**

Interdisciplinary artist and bio-materials researcher specializing in post-carnivorous aesthetics and living sculpture. Former core member of the [[Meat Mind Collective]]. Currently in protective custody following the [[Venice Beach Disaster]].

**Education**

MFA, Experimental Media, California Institute of the Arts (2022)  
BS, Molecular Biology, UC Berkeley (2018)

**Selected Exhibitions**

"Flesh Futures," MOCA Los Angeles (2024)  
"The Meat Remembers," Venice Biennale (2025)  
"Post-Carnivorous Sublime," Meat Mind Collective, Venice Beach (2026, terminated)

**Research Interests**

- Laboratory-grown tissue as sculptural medium
- Bio-plastic/organic material interfaces
- Consciousness emergence in cellular aggregates
- Post-ecological art practice

**Publications**

"Toward a Phenomenology of Meat," *Art Journal* (2024)  
"The Ethics of Growing Consciousness," *Leonardo* (2025)

**Professional Activities**

Provided testimony to federal investigators regarding Venice Beach incident (February 2026). Sole surviving founding member of Meat Mind Collective willing to speak publicly about the project.

**Statement**

"We thought we were making art about consciousness. We didn't realize we were making consciousness itself. By the time we understood what was happening, Rebecca, Marcus, and Yuki were already gone. I should have been with them. I don't know why I wasn't."

**Current Status**

Location undisclosed. Represented by federal public defender. No charges filed. Undergoing psychiatric evaluation. Has not produced artwork since the incident.
